About This Site

What this is. A live, browser-based feed of open-source news relevant to protective intelligence: targeted violence, civil unrest, executive/travel risk, infrastructure disruption, civil emergencies, and public health signals.

Where the data comes from. The GDELT Project's open news index (no login, no cost) by default, plus any RSS feeds added in Feed Manager. Every visitor's own browser pulls this data directly — nothing routes through a private server.

How it's processed. Headlines are grouped into "field reports" when multiple outlets cover the same story, and tagged HIGH/MED/LOW severity using keyword heuristics. This is a triage aid to help you scan volume and direction quickly — not a verified intelligence assessment. Always confirm anything you plan to act on.

Refresh cadence. Automatic pull every 60 seconds, visible in the countdown top-right. Manual refresh is always available.

Limits worth knowing. Flags and "new" tracking reset if you reload the page. Added feeds also reset on reload — use Export/Import Config in Feed Manager to carry your setup between sessions.

RSS feeds are fetched through a public read-only CORS relay (api.allorigins.win) since browsers block direct cross-origin XML reads. If a feed fails consistently, check its status in the table — it may block relays or require auth.
